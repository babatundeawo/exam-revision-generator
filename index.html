Overview

Upload your e-notes once. Generate exams for the whole session.

One Claude Project, dedicated to exams and revision files, can hold e-notes for as many subjects and classes as a school needs. State the Term, Subject and Class, say whether it should be an exam or a revision file, and it does the rest.

Not yet tailored to English Language or Mathematics (Mathematics especially, because of diagrams). Hold off on those two subjects until a tailored version is ready, every other subject works fully today.

Exam requests

“Generate First Term Examination for Physics SS2.” Produces a question paper, Section A: 30 objective questions, Section B: theory, plus a separate Marking Guide. 70 marks total, two .docx files.

Revision file requests

“Generate a revision file for Second Term, Physics, SS2.” Produces one Word document: summary notes for every topic, plus practice questions with full answers underneath each one.

Never guesses

The Term, Subject and Class must always be stated, along with exam or revision file. If anything is missing or unclear, or more than one e-note plausibly matches, it asks you to confirm rather than guessing.

A fresh paper every time

Question selection, angle and phrasing vary across requests, even for the same term, subject and class, so repeat generations don't produce an identical paper.

Exam composition rules

How marks are split across terms

A First or Second Term request draws 100% of both sections from that single term's e-note. A Third Term request blends all three terms in a fixed ratio.

SourceObjective (Section A)Theory (Section B)
1st Term e-note5 questions1 question
2nd Term e-note5 questions1 question
3rd Term e-note20 questions4 questions (3 regular + 1 extra)
Total30 questions6 questions offered

Section A: 30 questions, 1 mark each, exactly 4 options lettered A to D, answer letters randomly and evenly distributed. Section B: 1st/2nd term papers offer 5 questions and candidates answer any 4 (10 marks each); 3rd term papers offer 6 questions and candidates answer any 5 (8 marks each). Total marks always come to 70.

Formatting, every file

Consistent, letterhead-ready documents

Question paper

Your school's name and address, subject, class and term, centred and bold, followed by blank Name/Date/Time lines and the candidate instructions. Times New Roman 11pt, narrow 0.5″ margins, kept within 2 pages where possible.

Marking guide

A simpler centred title, the Section A answer key listed compactly, then every Section B question repeated in full above its model answer, with marks allocated per subpart. No page limit.

Get this tool

Set it up in three steps

01

Create a Project

One Project dedicated to exams and revision files, named something like “Exam & Revision Generator.” It can hold materials for every subject and class you need.

02

Edit two placeholders, then paste

Before pasting the instructions below into your Project's instructions field, replace {SCHOOL_NAME} with your school's full official name, and {SCHOOL_ADDRESS} with its address. Both appear on every generated exam header.

03

Upload your e-notes

Name files clearly so Claude can match them, e.g. Physics_SS2_FirstTerm.docx. JSS3/SS3 only need 1st and 2nd term uploaded, there is no 3rd term exam for those classes; every other class needs all three terms.
